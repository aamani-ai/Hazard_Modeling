# Wind pipeline — assumptions register

Every input / simplification / curve the wind build rests on, tracked explicitly, by layer. `AWN-*` =
wind-scoped (distinct from hail's `A*` and wildfire's `AW-*`). **Status** legend: `decided` · `assumed` ·
`to-verify` (must be checked *during* the layer) · `deferred` (known gap, revisit later) · `confirmed`
(evidence-backed).

## M1 — catalog *(stubs — detailed at M1)*

| # | Assumption | Basis / why | Status | Revisit when |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| AWN-15 | 🟡 **ASCE RP surface = a pre-integrated EVT product** — strong-wind M1 is **profile-assembly**, reading gust-by-MRI directly (no λ-fit, no dispersion test, no spatial factor); the RP→gust relation IS the pre-computed return-level curve. *Pre-integration is a borrow, not a free lunch* — inherits ASCE/NIST's assumptions, vintage, Exposure-C basis. **Generating method now pinned:** non-hurricane (our inland sites) = NIST **2-D Poisson-process / peaks-over-threshold** EVT + local regression, **bounded** tail (reverse-Weibull / POT-GPD, **ξ<0**; Simiu 1996, Peterka-Shahid 1998) — **not** block/annual-maxima; hurricane (a *separate* peril) = ARA Monte-Carlo simulation. **Deep-tail (App-F 10⁴–10⁶ MRI) standard error ≈ 10–16 mph** — large, compounding the M3-curve uncertainty | [DD-WN-3](decisions.md); [learning_logs/09](../../learning_logs/09_pre_integrated_vs_extracted_catalog.md) · [learning_logs/11](../../learning_logs/11_return_period_conventions.md) (EVT method + citations) | **decided (v1)** — the spine; SPC extraction = the documented cross-check | a public stochastic catalog appears, or the ASCE vintage/terrain basis is wrong for a site |
| AWN-16 | **Strong-wind λ ⇒ well-populated MC; tornado λ ⇒ sparse MC** — strong wind high-frequency (full tail); tornado rare per site → report SE, precision-matched known-answer tolerance, **TVaR alongside VaR** (VaR floors to $0 in the sparse tail) | [DD-WN-5](decisions.md); [learning_logs/10](../../learning_logs/10_monte_carlo_effective_sample_size.md) | decided · to-realize M1/M4 | tornado λ so low even large M leaves the near tail empty (note the squeeze) |
| AWN-17 | **Severity shape is per sub-peril (continuous 3-s-gust magnitude, unlike wildfire's 6 discrete FLP classes).** **Tornado = bounded GPD** on 3-s gust above μ, truncated at L (**ξ < 0**, short upper tail reaching the EF5 ceiling); analytic solve `ξ=(μ_mean−μ)/(μ_mean−L)`, `σ=−ξ(L−μ)`. **Strong wind = Gumbel / light-exponential (ξ ≈ 0), physically capped at L.** **Nuance (corrected):** ASCE's non-hurricane *generating* distribution **is** bounded (POT-GPD / reverse-Weibull, ξ<0; AWN-15) — but over the MRIs we read (≤10⁶) its return-level curve is **near-log-linear (R²≈0.999)** (the bound bites only far beyond our range), so our **ξ≈0 + hard cap at L** is a faithful *approximation* of that bounded tail, **not** a claim the tail is unbounded. The earlier "it is NOT a bounded GPD" was an overstatement about the curve's *shape over our range*, not the generating distribution. **ξ<0-with-the-analytic-solve is the tornado modeling rule**; for strong wind we read the curve's shape (≈log-linear) directly. | [DD-WN-8](decisions.md); [hazard_math/05](../../../Learning/ML-DL/InfraSure_related/hazard_math/05_extreme_value_theory_tail_modeling.md); [learning_logs/11](../../learning_logs/11_return_period_conventions.md) | decided · built M1 | a measured-gust record long enough to fit the tail directly (could move L=113 m/s or the strong-wind ξ≈0 assumption) |
| AWN-18 | **μ_mean fit to observed gusts (SPC / Storm-Events), NOT back-solved from an NRI EAL** — rejecting the old repo's EAL-anchored reverse-engineering of the magnitude distribution | old-repo `mag_sim` weakness; [DD-WN-8](decisions.md) | decided (v1) | a credible direct gust fit is available |
| AWN-19 | **Stationary λ** (current-climate) — climate trend / `λ(t)` not modeled; flag the **eastward drift** of tornado activity into the Southeast ("Dixie Alley") rather than freezing the climatology | Hazard-Data-Ref accuracy §7; mirrors hail/wildfire | deferred | asset-life-matched climate conditioning |
